The non-negotiable rule

AI drafts. Sansi approves. Every automation produces a draft you review before it leaves your hands. We will never build a flow that auto-sends to a client. That is not a feature — it is a liability we refuse to assume.

The reason is simple. The value of your platform is your judgement, not the speed of the machine. If the AI replies directly to a $2M enquiry and gets the tone wrong, you've lost the deal and damaged the brand. If the AI drafts and you take fifteen seconds to approve, you've kept your judgement in the loop and bought back the four hours you would have spent composing from scratch. Speed without judgement is liability. Speed with judgement is operational sophistication.

B · Accounts and credentials — actual build blockers
vii

Domain registrar access

We need to know which registrar holds sansi.africa, not just who manages DNS. We do not need to transfer ownership, but we do need the correct registrar login or a live handoff session to point the records to Vercel.

viii

Zoho account created in your name

Zoho CRM and Zoho Mail need to sit under your ownership. We can configure them, structure them, and connect them, but we cannot create or own the account for you.

ix

WhatsApp Business Account

WhatsApp Cloud API cannot run on a personal active WhatsApp number. It needs a dedicated business number and a verified Facebook Business Manager account before Meta can approve the application.

x

Google Analytics in your name

The analytics property should be owned by you from day one. We can create the measurement setup during a handoff session and connect it to the site.

C · Decisions that shape the architecture — needed by end of week 2
xi

Passphrase model

Choose one passphrase per property or one passphrase per buyer. Per-property is simpler and faster. Per-buyer gives better control and visibility, but adds more operational complexity.

xii

Property manager Ricky decision

Decide by the end of week 2 whether Ricky's property management business links into your platform. If yes, we include the lightweight module in Phase 2. If this changes later, it costs more because the architecture will already be set.

xiii

Newsletter timing

Decide whether the newsletter is live from day one in stealth or held until public launch. Both are valid; the choice changes how we treat signups, privacy language, and CRM routing.

xiv

Team email handles

Names and preferred format for the three joiners: first@sansi.africa, firstname.lastname@sansi.africa, or role-based addresses. We set this before Zoho Mail goes live.

xv

Team onboarding list

Names, roles, start dates, and expected CRM access for each person joining you. We pre-configure roles so they walk in with the right permissions, not a generic login.

D · Legal and compliance — flagged now so they are not launch-day surprises
